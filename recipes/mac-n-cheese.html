Mac N Cheese

Mac and cheese is a rich and creamy dish consisting of macaroni pasta mixed with a cheesy sauce.
It's particularly popular in the United States.

Ingredients

  • Elbow macaroni or other pasta shapes (2 cups or 200 grams)
  • Cheddar cheese (shredded, 2 cups or 200 grams)
  • Milk (2 cups or 480 ml)
  • Unsalted butter (4 tablespoons or 56 grams)
  • All-purpose flour (1/4 cup or 30 grams)
  • Mustard powder (1/2 teaspoon)
  • Salt (1/2 teaspoon, or to taste)
  • Black pepper (1/4 teaspoon, or to taste)
  • Optional: additional cheese for topping or breadcrumbs for a crispy topping

Instructions

  1. Cook the macaroni pasta according to the package instructions, until it is al dente (firm but not overly soft). Drain the pasta and set it aside.
  2. In a large saucepan or pot, melt the unsalted butter over medium heat. Once melted, add the flour to the pot and whisk continuously to form a roux. Cook the roux for about 1-2 minutes until it turns slightly golden in color.
  3. Slowly pour in the milk while whisking constantly to incorporate it into the roux. Continue whisking until the mixture thickens and comes to a gentle simmer. This creates the base for the cheese sauce.
  4. Reduce the heat to low. Add the shredded cheddar cheese to the sauce, reserving a small portion for topping if desired. Stir the sauce gently until the cheese is completely melted and smooth.
  5. Add the mustard powder, salt, and black pepper to the cheese sauce, and stir well to combine. Taste the sauce and adjust the seasonings according to your preference.
  6. Add the cooked macaroni pasta to the cheese sauce, stirring until all the pasta is coated evenly. Ensure that the sauce coats the pasta thoroughly for a creamy and delicious result.
  7. If you prefer a baked mac and cheese with a crispy topping, transfer the mixture to a greased baking dish. Sprinkle the reserved shredded cheese or breadcrumbs over the top.
  8. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Place the baking dish in the preheated oven and bake for approximately 20-25 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and the cheese is bubbly. Skip this step if you prefer stovetop mac and cheese.
  9. Once cooked, remove the macaroni and cheese from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes. This will allow the sauce to thicken slightly before serving.
  10. Serve the macaroni and cheese hot as a comforting main dish or a side dish. It pairs well with a fresh salad or steamed vegetables. Enjoy your delicious homemade mac and cheese!
